Melanoma, also redundantly known as malignant melanoma, is a type of skin cancer that develops from the pigment-producing cells known as melanocytes. About 25% of melanomas develop from moles. Changes in a mole that can indicate melanoma include an increase in size, irregular edges, change in color, itchiness or skin breakdown. Melanoma is the most dangerous type of skin cancer. Globally, in 2012, it newly occurred in 232,000 people.In 2015, there were 3.1 million people with active disease, which resulted in 59,800 deaths.
